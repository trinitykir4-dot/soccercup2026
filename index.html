Soccer Cup 2026 — Concept Moodboard

Overarching theme soccer + USA, one per brand, with each brand's collectible concept as called out in the kick-off summary: ML card collectible with golden-card mystery, HM penalty shootout, PF jersey collection, SB hero character collecting soccer kit. This moodboard is a rough first pass on the three open questions — (1) cards that don't read as a referee warning, (2) a Golden item that lands richly, (3) differentiation across all four brands — not a proposal, just a starting point to react to.

1Three angles to close

Raised but unresolved in today's meeting. The concept below is built around solving all three in a single visual language.

Anna's concern

"Card" reads as warning

In a soccer context, a flat rectangular colored card reads as yellow/red — a referee warning. Risk of negative association for a positive collectible.

Solution: treat cards as premium Panini-style trading cards — ornate gold borders, illustrated subjects, holofoil edges, displayed in a vault. Visually distant from any warning-card silhouette.
Noel's concern

Golden Card must feel rich

Laying flat card rectangles on screen reads like a loyalty punch-card, not a rewarding collectible moment. Must feel valuable.

Solution: fanned spread on a luxurious velvet backdrop, with the Golden Card radiating holofoil bloom at the centerpiece. Layout works regardless of completion order.
Alison's concern

Differentiation across 4 brands

Same campaign, same legal constraints, but each brand must read as its own product, not a recolor.

Solution: one theme, four distinct visual DNAs (see matrix). Collectible mechanic changes per brand — cards / penalty goal / eagle kit / jersey locker.

2McLuck — primary concept

ML · Lucky Cards

Primary focus of this moodboard. Same mechanic as Alison's Stamp Card Collectible Quest, reframed visually as a premium trading-card vault. Directly closes Angles 1 + 2.

Why this reads as premium, not a warning

Six ornate cards displayed as a 2×3 grid inside a glass-cube vitrine on a pedestal — echoing the McLuck Piece of Cake layout. Each card carries a distinct soccer-moment illustration; the Golden Card (top-center) has a trophy as its subject, gold background, holofoil shimmer. Ornate gold filigree borders + illustrated content kill any referee-card association.

  • Angle 1 closed: framed ornate Panini-style collectibles with illustrated subjects ≠ a flat warning card. The silhouette reads as trading card, not referee card.
  • Angle 2 closed: Golden Card sits at the fixed top-center slot of the 2×3 grid regardless of which mission is completed last — spatial anchor, not sequence-based reveal.
  • 6 collectibles rule: 5 soccer-moment cards (kick, save, stadium, header, ball in motion) + 1 Golden trophy card = the set. Clear narrative: playing moments → trophy reveal.
  • Brand DNA locked: gold + emerald green + deep crimson palette, sparkles, glossy 3D render — matches the ML style reference.
  • Legally clean: generic silhouettes only, no real players, no FIFA/World Cup branding, no real trophy design.
How to read this image. Concept lockup showing full composition — banner (extending above the colored background into the transparent area, per the Piece of Cake pattern), Ultimate Reward plaque, hero scene with 6 cards inside the glass cube, CTA button and timer (extending below the background). In production each of those elements becomes a real brand component; the AI-rendered text/icons are placeholder. The cards themselves — 2×3 grid inside the vitrine, Golden Card at top-center with a trophy illustration — are the custom hero asset. CTA name: COLLECT (kept distinct from SCORE / SUIT UP / KICK OFF on other brands).

4Golden-item placement principle

Works for McLuck Golden Card, PlayFame Golden Jersey, and any "premium variant" asset added later.

Rule: the golden item has a compositional slot, not a sequential slot. Across brands — McLuck cards, HelloMillions X-markers, SpinBlitz kit pieces, PlayFame jerseys — one slot is reserved for the Golden variant (top-center card, center X, golden match ball, top-center jersey). All slots are visible from the start as locked silhouettes. The player unlocks them in any order — Golden lives at a fixed spatial anchor, so it always lands heroically regardless of which mission completes last. This addresses the constraint raised in the kick-off: quests can be completed in any order, so sequential "reveal last" logic is unsafe. Spatial anchoring is safe.
